# HR-Analytics-Employee-Attrition-and-Performance-PowerBI  
## About Project :  
Employee attrition analytics is specifically focused on identifying why employees voluntarily leave, what might have prevented them from leaving, and how we can use data to predict attrition risk. Most importantly, this type of employee predictive analytics can be used to help organizations understand and design the interventions that will be most effective in reducing unwanted attrition.  
## DataSet :  
https://lnkd.in/drH9eFKc  
## Insights :  
1- The company has 1,470 total employees, with 1,233 active employees and 237 employees who left, resulting in an attrition rate of 16.12%.

2- The Research & Development department experiences the highest impact in both workforce size and attrition, with 133 employees leaving, followed by Sales, while Human Resources has the smallest workforce and lowest attrition.

3- Laboratory Technicians, Sales Executives, and Research Scientists represent the roles with the highest employee turnover, indicating potential challenges in operational and technical positions.

4- The 18–29 age group shows the highest attrition rate, suggesting that younger employees are more likely to leave compared to other age groups.

5- Most employees who left the company have Life Sciences or Medical educational backgrounds, which also represent the largest portion of the workforce.

6- Attrition tends to occur during the early years of employment, especially within the first few years at the company.

7- The 30–39 age group represents the largest portion of active employees, indicating that mid-career professionals form the core of the organization’s workforce.

8- Job satisfaction ratings are generally moderate to high across most roles, especially among Sales  
## Recommendations  
1- Since the 30–39 age group represents the largest portion of active employees, the company should focus on career advancement opportunities and leadership development programs for this group to maintain engagement and retention.

2- Because the Research & Development department has the largest workforce, implementing continuous training, innovation programs, and recognition systems could help sustain productivity and employee satisfaction.

3- The company should monitor job satisfaction levels regularly, especially in large roles such as Sales Executives, Research Scientists, and Laboratory Technicians, to prevent potential future attrition.

4- Given the heavy reliance on Life Sciences educational backgrounds, the organization may consider diversifying recruitment strategies to bring in talent from different educational fields and perspectives.

5- Departments with smaller employee counts, such as Human Resources, should ensure balanced workloads and adequate staffing to avoid burnout.

6- Maintaining consistent performance evaluation systems across departments will help ensure fairness, transparency, and employee trust in the performance management process.
